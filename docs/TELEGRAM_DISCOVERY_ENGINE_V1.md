## Executive Conclusion

Telegram can become a valuable Fortune Shrine discovery and relationship channel, but the opportunity is narrower than “crypto users are on Telegram.”

The strongest environments are:

1. Small-to-medium trading chats where members discuss positions, risk, patience, drawdowns, and market conditions.
2. Perpetual and derivatives protocol communities with wallet-native users.
3. Crypto poker communities where uncertainty, waiting, loss, and emotional control are already normal subjects.
4. A few builder communities where launches and uncertainty are discussed candidly.

The weakest environments are:

- announcement-only channels;
- giant exchange groups dominated by support requests, campaigns, and spam;
- unofficial Polymarket bots and “new market” feeds;
- signal, pump, airdrop, and guaranteed-return groups;
- communities where members cannot speak.

### Important prediction-market finding

No healthy, high-confidence public Telegram community was verified for Polymarket, Kalshi, Manifold, or Metaculus.

- Polymarket’s public website points to its Discord, not an official Telegram community.
- No official Telegram link was found on the public Kalshi, Manifold, or Metaculus homepages.
- Search results carrying “Polymarket” were dominated by bots, market feeds, tiny placeholders, or unverifiable groups.

Therefore Telegram should not be treated as a direct Polymarket-community channel. It should be treated as a place to find adjacent wallet-native traders who already live with uncertainty.

## Scoring Method

Ranks estimate the probability of eventually producing an authentic Fortune Shrine customer, not immediate permission to promote.

Factors:

- Trust and evidence of authenticity
- Real member conversation
- Wallet familiarity
- Uncertainty and risk intensity
- Community helpfulness
- Ability to become a recognized human participant
- Whether a state-focused blessing could feel natural after trust exists

Scores are research judgments, not measured conversion rates.

## Policy Codes

- **P1 — Strict community:** Normal discussion is allowed; unsolicited promotion should be assumed prohibited.
- **P2 — Support/community:** Primarily support and product discussion. Off-topic posts and promotion are likely removed.
- **P3 — Independent chat:** Conversation appears open, but rules must be read after joining.
- **P4 — Announcement channel:** Members cannot freely post. Useful only for observation and discovering adjacent people/events.
- **P5 — Large exchange group:** Public chat exists, but spam, moderation, impersonation, and account-safety risks are high.

## Health Codes

Each code answers, in order:

1. Would a normal human enjoy spending time here?
2. Are there real conversations?
3. Are people helping one another?
4. Could a blessing eventually feel natural?
5. Could Fortune Shrine participate without looking like advertising?

Values: **Y** yes, **P** partly/unknown, **N** no.

## Entry Codes

### E1 — Trading conversation

- **Day 1–3:** Observe recurring members, language, moderator behavior, and moments where traders discuss uncertainty rather than price calls.
- **Day 4–7:** Answer ordinary questions or acknowledge a thoughtful risk-management observation. No links and no Shrine language.
- **Day 8+:** Build continuity with a few recognizable members. Only offer a state-focused blessing when the person’s language naturally invites it.

### E2 — Support/product community

- **Day 1–3:** Read rules and separate support traffic from genuine community conversation.
- **Day 4–7:** Participate only where personal experience or a useful answer is relevant.
- **Day 8+:** Maintain a low-frequency presence. Never redirect support conversations to Fortune Shrine.

### E3 — Poker/risk community

- **Day 1–3:** Observe tournament, bankroll, tilt, waiting, and post-loss language.
- **Day 4–7:** Join strategy or emotional-discipline discussions as a peer, never as a gambling promoter.
- **Day 8+:** Build relationships around composure, patience, and recovery—not winning or “luck.”

### E4 — Builder community

- **Day 1–3:** Learn what members are launching and which members consistently help others.
- **Day 4–7:** Give useful, concrete feedback and acknowledge launch anxiety without introducing the product.
- **Day 8+:** Become a familiar participant around difficult decisions, launches, setbacks, and persistence.

### E5 — Observation channel

- **Day 1–3:** Follow posts and identify linked discussions, authors, analysts, or events.
- **Day 4–7:** Continue observation outside the channel; do not attempt promotion in an announcement feed.
- **Day 8+:** Use the channel only as a discovery source for public conversations elsewhere.

### E6 — Large exchange group

- **Day 1–3:** Observe only. Verify official moderators and anti-scam warnings.
- **Day 4–7:** Participate only if a genuine non-promotional discussion exists.
- **Day 8+:** Keep activity extremely low. Never DM members, mention payments, or respond to crisis/support cases.

## Excluded Findings

The following were deliberately excluded:

- `@technicalroundup`: explicitly promises guaranteed payouts and signals.
- Polymarket market-feed bots and trading bots such as KreoPolyBot, PolyCop, and TradePolyBot.
- Pump, “whale calls,” airdrop, 100x, and guaranteed-return groups.
- Tiny or hijacked handles pretending to be well-known brands.
- Political/news communities where uncertainty is discussed but community purpose is unrelated.
- Gambling-addiction or crisis-support communities.

## Community-Type Verdict

### 1. Crypto derivatives and perpetuals — highest probability

Best current path:

`GMX trading chat → Deribit → Orderly → Gains → Santiment/CoinGlass`

Why:

- wallet familiarity is already present;
- uncertainty is immediate and repeated;
- members use natural language about waiting, risk, liquidation, restraint, and conviction;
- recurring identities make trust possible;
- a pause-oriented ritual can make sense without implying prediction.

### 2. Crypto poker — second highest probability

CoinPoker is the clearest wallet-native poker bridge. Poker naturally contains waiting, loss, courage, patience, and tilt, but the Shrine must never imply that a blessing improves results.

### 3. Prediction markets — strong theoretical fit, weak Telegram evidence

The user thesis is excellent, but the public Telegram infrastructure is currently weak. Polymarket’s verifiable community path points toward Discord. Overtime is the clearest Telegram-adjacent prediction/sports market community found.

### 4. Builders — high humanity, lower wallet readiness

Small founder groups may produce deeper conversations, but payment via USDC and the ritual framing require more explanation than with crypto-native users.

## Could Telegram Produce the First Customer Faster Than X?

**Possibly, but only through one or two small, healthy groups—not through broad Telegram coverage.**

Telegram advantages:

- repeated presence creates familiarity;
- conversation is less performative than X;
- members may discuss losses and uncertainty more candidly;
- wallet use is normal in crypto communities.

Telegram disadvantages:

- entering a group as a newcomer carries a trust deficit;
- links and product references quickly look like scams;
- many high-volume groups are dominated by support, bots, or promotions;
- the strongest prediction-market communities are not on Telegram;
- one wrong approach can result in an immediate ban.

Judgment:

- **For an immediate cold reply:** X is faster.
- **For a trusted first customer after 1–3 weeks:** a good Telegram trading group may outperform X.
- **For broad acquisition:** Telegram is not currently safer or easier than X.

## Community Type Most Likely to Produce the First Offering

1. **Crypto derivatives/perpetual traders**
2. **Crypto poker players**
3. **Prediction-market users**
4. **Builders**

The first Offering is most likely from someone who:

- already holds and spends crypto;
- has experienced a recent uncertain or costly moment;
- remains active and reflective;
- recognizes the value of a ritual pause;
- encounters Fortune Shrine through a familiar human, not an advertisement.

## Thirty-Minute Daily Telegram Routine

### Minutes 0–10: Observe two core groups

Rotate among:

- GMX Trading Chat
- Deribit
- Orderly
- Gains Network
- Santiment

Read only. Note recurring names and genuine state language.

### Minutes 10–20: Participate once

Add one useful, ordinary contribution:

- answer a factual community question when qualified;
- acknowledge a thoughtful risk or discipline point;
- ask a sincere follow-up question.

No links. No Shrine. No blessing formula.

### Minutes 20–27: Record one or two humans

Record:

- public username;
- community;
- exact public message;
- state: waiting/risk/loss/restraint/recovery;
- whether they are continuing forward;
- whether a future response would be safe and natural.

### Minutes 27–30: Review restraint

Ask:

- Did I help the community?
- Did I behave like a normal member?
- Would my message still be appropriate if Fortune Shrine did not exist?

If the answer is no, do not post.

## Recommended First Seven-Day Test

Do not enter 50 communities.

Start with:

1. GMX Trading Chat
2. Deribit
3. Orderly Discussion
4. Gains Network
5. Santiment

For seven days:

- join no more than two groups on the first day;
- observe before writing;
- make at most one normal contribution per group per day;
- do not mention Fortune Shrine;
- do not send links;
- do not DM;
- record whether real people respond or remember the account.

The first validation question is not:

> Can Telegram drive traffic?

It is:

> Can Fortune Shrine become a trusted human presence in one trading community?

## Evidence Notes

Primary evidence used:

- Public Telegram preview pages linked in the table, including public member/subscriber and online counts.
- Official public websites for Deribit, CoinGlass, CoinPoker, Gains Network, Glassnode, Jupiter, MUX, Orderly, and Santiment, which linked to the corresponding Telegram communities.
- Public homepages for Polymarket, Kalshi, Manifold, and Metaculus. Polymarket publicly linked Discord; no verified official Telegram links were found on these four public homepages.

Public counts, group ownership, rules, and activity can change. Before joining any group, verify:

- the link from the project’s official website;
- pinned rules;
- whether direct messages are prohibited;
- moderator identities;
- whether the group has recently changed ownership.
