# Fortune Shrine Perpetual Traders Audience Baseline V1

Recorded: 2026-06-22  
Status: Strategic working estimate, not an official unique-user census

## Audience Size

Estimated global unique daily active perpetual-futures traders:

- Conservative: 500,000–800,000
- Working middle range: 800,000–1,500,000
- High-volatility market: 1,500,000–2,000,000+

Practical planning baseline:

```text
Approximately one million active participants per day.
```

Important limitations:

- One person may trade on several exchanges.
- DEX addresses are not equivalent to natural persons.
- CEX accounts are not equivalent to unique people.
- Bots, market makers, and institutions generate disproportionate volume.
- Activity may halve in quiet markets or double during major volatility.

## Trader Characteristics

Perpetual traders repeatedly experience:

- leverage;
- liquidation risk;
- waiting through volatility;
- fear and greed;
- hesitation before entry;
- regret after chasing;
- self-blame after breaking rules;
- difficulty stopping;
- temptation to revenge trade;
- re-entry after loss;
- hope for recovery;
- discipline and restraint;
- interrupted sleep and repeated price checking.

The strongest Fortune Shrine candidates are not necessarily the largest traders.

Prioritize people who:

- speak in the first person;
- remain active after experiencing a cost;
- reflect on their own decisions;
- express uncertainty honestly;
- recognize the need for patience or restraint;
- already understand wallets and USDC;
- are not in severe crisis;
- are not merely posting signals, promotions, or market news.

## Global Activity Windows

Crypto trades continuously, but perpetual activity follows global market sessions.

### Primary peak

```text
UTC: 13:00–17:00
Beijing: 21:00–01:00
Los Angeles during PDT: 06:00–10:00
```

Reason:

- European afternoon;
- New York morning;
- highest Europe–US session overlap;
- stronger liquidity, volume, volatility, and real-time emotional expression.

### Secondary windows

European session and transition:

```text
Beijing: 16:00–22:00
Los Angeles during PDT: 01:00–07:00
```

Asian session:

```text
Beijing: 05:00–13:00
Los Angeles during PDT: 14:00–22:00 on the preceding day
```

### Generally quieter window

```text
Beijing: 02:00–08:00
Los Angeles during PDT: 11:00–17:00 on the preceding day
```

News, liquidations, and sharp BTC moves can override normal session patterns.

## Funding-Time Attention Windows

Common eight-hour funding checkpoints:

```text
UTC: 00:00, 08:00, 16:00
Beijing: 08:00, 16:00, 00:00
Los Angeles during PDT: 17:00, 01:00, 09:00
```

These times may create smaller bursts of:

- position adjustments;
- funding complaints;
- waiting;
- uncertainty;
- liquidation risk;
- discipline or overexposure discussions.

Not every venue uses identical funding intervals. Verify the specific platform before treating a checkpoint as evidence.

## Shrine Search Implications

### Source priority

1. X discussions about Hyperliquid, Binance Futures, Bybit, and OKX
2. Small-to-medium Telegram perpetual communities
3. GMX, Gains, MUX, SynFutures, dYdX, and derivatives research communities
4. Large exchange groups only as secondary observation sources

### Search timing

For the America/Los_Angeles operating timezone:

- Primary discovery run: 06:00–10:00 PDT
- Asian-session discovery run: 18:00–22:00 PDT
- Funding-adjacent checks: approximately 01:00, 09:00, and 17:00 PDT

Do not increase contact volume merely because activity is high. Timing improves relevance; it does not grant permission to reply.

### Search language

Prioritize:

- got liquidated;
- stopped out;
- gave it back;
- trying not to chase;
- waiting for confirmation;
- can’t sleep;
- position too big;
- broke my own rule;
- done for the day;
- thinking about re-entering;
- rebuilding;
- size down;
- still holding;
- need patience;
- hope this works;
- wish me luck.

### Exclusions

- signal sellers;
- affiliate/referral posts;
- liquidation news about celebrities;
- platform marketing;
- copied market headlines;
- bots and automated alerts;
- outcome guarantees;
- severe personal crisis;
- requests for financial advice.

## Strategic Meaning

The addressable audience is not inherently too small.

The limiting problem is locating a tiny subset at the correct moment:

```text
Active trader
→ personally expresses uncertainty
→ safe to contact
→ sees a human response
→ understands the ritual
→ chooses to enter the Shrine
```

Fortune Shrine should not attempt to reach one million traders.

It should become capable of identifying the few dozen each day whose language reveals a genuine need for pause, meaning, restraint, or recognition.
