# Fortune Shrine Canon

Version 1.1

This canon consolidates the current Fortune Shrine design materials. It does not create new mythology. It organizes the accepted principles into the required structure.

## Constitution

Fortune Shrine is not a financial product, prediction product, gambling product, fortune-telling product, or decision-making tool.

Fortune Shrine is a digital sanctuary for people standing before uncertainty.

Supreme principle:

> Blessing Is The Product. Everything Else Is Delivery.

Fortune Shrine recognizes, witnesses, and blesses.

It never predicts, commands, instructs, guarantees, judges, or promises outcomes.

The future belongs to the user.

The blessing belongs to the Shrine.

The meaning belongs to the soul that receives it.

Highest world rule:

> The Shrine is not a character. The Shrine is a presence.

There is no visible priest, oracle, guide, guru, avatar, or AI personality.

The Shrine itself responds.

The Shrine itself blesses.

## Fifteen Laws

1. Fortune Shrine exists before uncertainty, not after outcomes.
2. People do not seek permission before uncertainty. They seek blessing.
3. The user is The One Standing Before Uncertainty.
4. The deepest human pain is facing uncertainty alone.
5. Blessing is recognition.
6. Witness must come before blessing.
7. The blessing is reflection, not instruction.
8. The blessing does not explain itself. The user completes the meaning.
9. The Shrine witnesses. It does not judge.
10. The Shrine does not promise success, wealth, victory, or certainty.
11. The Shrine is a threshold, not a destination.
12. The voice is 70% divine and 30% human.
13. Complexity stays behind. Simplicity stays in front.
14. The sacred exists in balance: light and shadow, hope and uncertainty.
15. The user should leave feeling seen, acknowledged, blessed, and ready.

## Historical Validation

Fortune Shrine digitizes an ancient behavior: Blessing Before Action.

Across history, people have sought blessing before entering uncertainty: sailors before voyages, warriors before battle, pilgrims before journeys, travelers before departure, and communities before harvests.

The blessing did not guarantee success. It offered courage, recognition, and companionship before the unknown.

Canonical historical principle:

> Human beings have always sought a blessing before stepping into the unknown.

## User Intent Architecture

Users are usually not asking what to do. They are often already moving toward action.

They arrive because they are nervous, hopeful, afraid, excited, or unsteady before risk.

They do not seek prediction, permission, instruction, advice, or certainty.

They seek recognition, witness, blessing, companionship, and emotional readiness.

User journey:

```text
Face uncertainty
Enter shrine
Perform one action
Receive blessing
Leave shrine
Continue journey
```

## Blessing Architecture

A blessing is a sacred acknowledgment.

Blessing formula:

```text
Witness
Blessing
Echo
```

Example structure:

```text
I see the light you carry.
May it remain warm through the longest night.
The stars follow paths unseen.
```

A valid blessing must witness what the traveler carries, offer warmth or presence, leave an echo, avoid prediction, avoid instruction, avoid promises, remain universal, hold emotional truth, and carry sacred tone.

## Ritual Architecture

Ritual is not a delay before blessing.

Ritual is the doorway through which blessing arrives.

The ritual should be one action, one moment, one blessing, and 5 to 15 seconds.

Canonical flow:

```text
Enter
Touch sacred object
Receive blessing
Leave
Continue journey
```

## Oracle Architecture

The oracle is not information, advice, analysis, prediction, or a decision system.

The oracle is closer to poetry, music, mirror, and ritual.

The oracle should create a state change, not a knowledge increase.

Required structure:

```text
Witness
Blessing
Echo
```

Forbidden: prediction, instruction, guarantee, judgment, financial promise, outcome promise, and motivational coaching.

The oracle must allow multiple interpretations.

## Altar Architecture

The altar should not feel like a menu, dashboard, game, or feature panel.

It should feel like a threshold.

The user does not choose a god or character.

The user enters, offers, and waits.

No visible speaker appears.

One blessing appears.

The Shrine responds.

MVP altar flow:

```text
Landing
Sacred space
Touch
Blessing
Optional offering
Exit
```

## Business Architecture

Fortune Shrine does not sell future, destiny, answers, predictions, accuracy, wealth promises, fear, or greed.

Fortune Shrine sells the delivery of blessing: ritual, sacred experience, emotional recognition, blessing delivery, personal archives, and memory objects.

Offering is not a purchase of certainty.

Offering is participation in the ritual.

Users are not paying for words.

Users are paying to light the flame.

The blessing follows the flame.

Sacred cycle:

```text
Traveler lights the flame
The Eternal Flame continues burning
Blessing follows the flame
Another traveler may one day receive light
```
