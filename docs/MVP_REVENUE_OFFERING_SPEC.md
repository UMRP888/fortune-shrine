# MVP Revenue & Offering Specification

Version 1.1

## Important Correction

Do not present offerings as raw price tiers.

Avoid:

- 1 USDC
- 3 USDC
- 5 USDC
- 10 USDC
- Custom

Raw amounts feel transactional, cheap, and too close to a tip jar.

The user should not feel:

```text
I am buying content.
```

The user should feel:

```text
I am participating in a ritual.
I am lighting the flame.
I am helping future travelers.
```

## Pricing Principle

Do not lead with price.

Lead with meaning.

Price is secondary.

Ritual is primary.

## Correct Offering Structure

Show:

```text
Offering Name
Meaning
Price
```

Canonical MVP offerings:

| Offering | Meaning | Price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Traveler's Offering | A small flame for the journey. | 5 USDC |
| Keeper's Offering | Help the flame endure through the night. | 15 USDC |
| Sacred Offering | A brighter light for future travelers. | 35 USDC |
| Eternal Offering | Help keep the Shrine burning. | 88 USDC |

## Ritual Flow

```text
Arrival
Choose An Offering
Light The Flame
The offering is received
The Eternal Flame grows brighter
Blessing follows the flame
Continue Journey
```

## Forbidden Design

Avoid:

- raw dollar tiers
- custom amount fields
- donation page patterns
- tip jar patterns
- microtransaction patterns
- cheap content-purchase framing

## Forbidden Language

Do not use:

- Donate
- Upgrade
- Premium
- VIP
- Unlock
- Exclusive
- Legendary
- Whale

Preferred:

- Traveler's Offering
- Keeper's Offering
- Sacred Offering
- Eternal Offering
- Light The Flame
- Keep The Flame Burning
- For The Next Traveler

## After Offering

The response must feel sacred, not transactional.

Never say:

- Payment Successful
- Thank You
- Receipt Generated

Preferred:

- The offering is received.
- The Eternal Flame grows brighter.
- Another flame now burns within the Shrine.
- May your offering travel farther than your sight.
- The Shrine remembers your kindness.

## Revenue Validation Principle

The MVP should test:

```text
Will users pay for participation in a sacred ritual?
```

Not:

```text
Will users pay for generated text?
```

## Canonical Statement

Users are not paying for words.

Users are paying to light the flame.

The blessing follows the flame.

Fortune Shrine is not a content product.

Fortune Shrine is a ritual product.
