# Pantheon Notes

This file records the first pantheon direction for Fortune Shrine.

The guardians are real mythological or religious figures from different cultures.

The product may use two kinds of lines:

- historically sourced or traditionally attributed lines, when they are clearly appropriate and respectful
- original Shrine oracle lines, created in the symbolic voice of that guardian

The product does not need to over-explain this distinction to the user. The user is not here for academic proof. The user is here for comfort, ritual, and an interpretable blessing before uncertainty.

Internally, do not falsely label an original line as a verified historical quote. Externally, keep the experience simple: one guardian, one blessing, one oracle.

## Copy Rules

Never write a line that gives trading advice or implies certainty.

Forbidden:

- buy
- sell
- enter
- exit
- invest
- wait
- act now
- guaranteed
- profit
- returns
- winning
- this will happen
- the god guarantees
- the god commands

Preferred:

- May...
- Perhaps...
- A door...
- The road...
- The wind...
- The cup...
- The mirror...
- The threshold...
- Something opens...
- Something watches...
- The sign is not yet complete...
- The answer remains veiled...

The oracle should never explain itself. The user completes the meaning.

## Comfort Rule

The user has paid for a moment of emotional steadiness.

The line should feel like:

- comfort without certainty
- hope without promise
- ambiguity without emptiness
- dignity without instruction
- mystery without threat

Good oracle lines are slightly vague, symbolic, and generous. They give the user room to project meaning.

Avoid lines that feel too clever, too cold, too negative, too academic, or too obviously generated.

## Research Sources To Continue From

Use reputable references when expanding the pantheon, such as:

- Encyclopaedia Britannica
- World History Encyclopedia
- Metropolitan Museum of Art essays
- Museum and university mythology references
- Official cultural institution pages

When adding a guardian, record:

- name
- culture or region
- symbolic domains
- what the voice should feel like
- what the voice must never imply
