# Fortune Shrine Canon

Version 2026-06-12

This document exists so the Shrine can survive interruptions: account problems, lost chat context, new sessions, new collaborators, or long pauses.

If anything conflicts with this document, the Constitution wins.

## Supreme Constitution

Fortune Shrine is a digital blessing ritual before uncertainty.

It is not a prediction product.
It is not a trading signal.
It is not a gambling charm.
It is not a fortune-telling system.
It is not financial advice.
It is not therapy.

Supreme rule:

```text
Bless the state.
Never bless the outcome.
```

The Shrine may bless courage, restraint, patience, clarity, steadiness, recovery, humility, and the ability to pause.

The Shrine must never bless profit, winning, direction, returns, revenge, escape, certainty, or a specific result.

Forbidden meaning:

```text
May this trade win.
May this coin moon.
May you recover everything.
May the market favor you.
The Shrine says buy.
The Shrine says sell.
```

Allowed meaning:

```text
May your hand stay steady before the candle moves.
May you know the difference between courage and hunger.
May the loss not take your name from you.
May restraint remain beside you when the room grows loud.
```

## Core User

The first and primary users are people standing before risk:

- crypto futures traders
- meme coin traders
- contract players
- gamblers and gambling-adjacent risk takers
- day traders and options traders
- people who just lost, chased, froze, overtraded, or want to enter again

They may not say they need a blessing.

They may say:

- I need luck.
- I need to calm down.
- I am about to ape in.
- I am about to revenge trade.
- I cannot stop watching the candle.
- I got liquidated.
- I need one clean breath before the next move.

The Shrine does not encourage the move.

The Shrine creates a ritual pause before the move.

## Product Position

Fortune Shrine occupies the space between:

- trading communities that are full of urgency
- fortune systems that promise certainty
- wellness products that feel too clean or detached
- gambling rituals that can become destructive

The Shrine should feel like:

```text
Before you enter the trade, light the flame.
```

But it must never become:

```text
Light the flame to win the trade.
```

## The Flame

The Flame is the Shrine.

The flame is not decoration.
The flame is not a reward effect.
The flame is not a game object.
The flame is not a loading indicator.

The flame is the visible presence of the Shrine.

It should feel alive, but never animated.
It should respond, but never celebrate.
It should acknowledge, but never perform.

Idle flame:

- subtle movement
- subtle brightness variation
- no visible loop
- no mechanical pulse
- no jackpot or reward energy

Blessing response:

- slight brightening
- slight height increase
- soft environmental response
- slow return to stillness

Forbidden:

- bursts
- flashes
- explosions
- golden eruptions
- reward particles
- fantasy spell effects
- dramatic visual climax

- old
- quiet
- warm

- dark

- sacred without being religious

The Shrine should not feel:

- futuristic

- astrological
- marketing-like
- SaaS-like
- casino-like
- motivational
- decorative

Visual hierarchy:

```text
Flame
Altar
Inscription
Words
Interface
```

The user should first see fire.
Then stone.
Then discover the inscription.
Then read.
Then act.

## Altar Inscription

The inscription is not a button.
The inscription is not typography.
The inscription is not UI text.

The inscription is a relic.

The altar should appear to contain the words.
The viewer should feel the words have always been there.

Direction:

- single-line inscription is acceptable
- hidden in plain sight
- slightly damaged
- not too bright
- not too readable
- stone texture and weathering should pass through the letters
- light should feel trapped inside carved grooves

The inscription should be discovered, not announced.

## Blessing Language

Every blessing follows:

```text
Witness
Blessing
Echo
```

Witness acknowledges the state.
Blessing offers warmth.
Echo leaves an image.

Blessings must be:

- non-predictive
- non-instructional
- non-judgmental
- emotionally true
- simple enough to feel immediate
- poetic enough to feel sacred

Avoid:

- trading advice
- therapy language
- motivational slogans
- financial certainty
- outcome promises
- moral judgment
- shame

Preferred trader-state domains:

- waiting
- risk
- loss
- return
- discipline
- restraint
- overexposure
- revenge
- liquidation
- patience
- greed
- fear
- re-entry

Examples of valid blessing directions:

```text
Waiting can make the hand restless.
May patience remain stronger than hunger.
The flame does not chase the wind.
```

```text
Loss can make the room very loud.
May your name remain larger than what was taken.
Ash still remembers fire.
```

```text
The candle moves without asking.
May your breath return before your hand follows.
Some doors are not meant to be entered quickly.
```

The Shrine does not sell plans.

Offering paths:

Meaning must come first.

Allowed framing:

- A blessing carried forward.
- A light for those who follow.

- support us

- buy
- upgrade
- plan
- tier
- package
- unlock

## Propagation Principle

Users may not spread the product.

Users may spread the blessing.

The growth system should study:

- why people share blessings
- which blessings travel
- when traders share ritual screenshots
- how loss, risk, restraint, and recovery language spreads

Do not spam communities.
Do not fake participation.
Do not auto-post into groups.
Do not turn the Shrine into a marketing account.

Low-frequency, high-quality presence is the correct mode.
